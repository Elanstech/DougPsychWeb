About Dr. Doug Uhlig

A Unique Approach to Psychological Wellness

Our Philosophy

Philosophy and Approach

As a Positive Psychologist and Teacher of Transcendental Meditation, I focus on the "Psyche" or soul in addressing family and individual challenges.

I address issues related to trauma, abandonment, addiction, career, and relationships. My approach helps individuals manage emotions, anxiety, and depression while increasing self-awareness and consciousness about behavioral impact on creating change.

What I enjoy most about my work is witnessing profound existential transformations. Through deep awareness work, my clients can naturally alleviate depression and anxiety, prevent addiction, and discover their true passion and purpose in life.

Our Approach

How We're Different

My uniqueness as a psychologist stems from diverse life experience. As both a psychotherapist and life/business coach, I offer comprehensive support for psychological healing, happiness, career guidance, and relationship success.

My practice style is goal-oriented, combining Positive Psychology, Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, Gestalt, and family therapy with mindfulness and meditation. This holistic approach improves mood, awareness, and behavior.

In therapy sessions, I create an environment of caring and unconditional acceptance. My gentle curiosity and respectful approach help clients feel heard and understood, leading to enhanced self-awareness and decision-making abilities.

Our Story

Biography and Mission

Early Journey

After military service in Southeast Asia, I lived in Tokyo, studying Japanese, Zen Buddhism, and Aikido. This led to becoming a teacher of Transcendental Meditation in Switzerland under Maharishi Mahesh Yogi.

Professional Development

My journey includes roles as a business entrepreneur, military officer, artist, and human resources consultant. This diverse experience enriches my psychological practice and understanding of human nature.

Education & Expertise

Holding degrees from prestigious institutions including a Ph.D. from Walden University, my research focused on Mindfulness Meditation for substance abuse prevention.
